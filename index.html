Session 2 • Hands-on · what 'good' looks like

A 7-day plan, done well

Day 1Read the summary + key concepts — skim the source with citations open.
Day 2–3Deep-dive the difficult terms & core sections — ask “explain with an example”.
Day 4Active recall — flashcards, self-test without notes.
Day 5Practice — attempt the MCQs and viva questions aloud.
Day 6Consolidate — rewrite the one-page notes from memory.
Day 7Simulate — audio overview on the commute + a final gap check.

A good plan is document-specific and uses varied methods — not “read everything again”.

Session 2 • Best practice

Use AI like a topper, not a shortcut

do

Verify & rewrite

Check answers against cited passages; rewrite key outputs in your own words; use flashcards for active recall; ask follow-ups until you can explain it to a friend.

don't

Don't outsource thinking

Don't submit text you haven't read; don't trust numbers / formulas without checking the source; don't upload confidential or copyrighted material; don't let tools replace practice problems.

Tools compress reading time — understanding still happens in your head.
